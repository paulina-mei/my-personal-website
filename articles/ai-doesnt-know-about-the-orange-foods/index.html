AI doesn't know about the orange foods

March 8, 2026

Your humanness is your superpower.

There's an undercurrent of excitement and fear with the rise of AI. As agentic AI gets better and can do more, content is going to get more and more commoditized. The people who come out on top will be the ones who double down on their specific flaws, interests, and strengths — the things that make them irreducibly themselves.

Let's remember who has opposable thumbs and who doesn't. If you could see me, I'm pointing at myself (and at you). You exist. Your life is finite. What you choose to prioritize is yours alone. The way our brains process the same feelings, sights, and sounds — wildly different from person to person. That's not a bug. That's the whole point.

Lately I've had this feeling of malaise. Being mentally unwell is different from being physically unwell — there's no clear path to a cure. I can't just take an antibiotic and feel better. AI can suggest breathing exercises and staying distracted, but human problems require human solutions. AI doesn't know that when I'm feeling down, I like making a rendition of hong shao rou. It doesn't know that when I don't want to cook, I eat meals made entirely of orange foods.

Sentimentality is a deep, profound human emotion. So is joy. AI can predict trends, but it can't carry them out where it matters — in the real world, in a body, in a life that's yours. You need real people for that.

Go touch grass. Not as a joke. As a reminder.
